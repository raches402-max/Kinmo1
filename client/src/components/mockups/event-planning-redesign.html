Current vs. Proposed Flow

❌ Current Flow (7+ steps)

1 Navigate to Create Event tab
2 Choose between AI/Manual paths
3 Browse venues in Explore tab
4 Add venues to sidebar
5 Choose Quick Schedule vs Build & Edit
6 Review in modal
7 Confirm and send

✓ Proposed Flow (2-3 steps)

1 Click "Create Event" or "Surprise Me"
2 Review inline — edit if needed
3 Send invites
Key difference: No modals, no tab switching, no confusion about which path to take. Everything happens in one place.

Design Principles

  • Timeline as truth — One view shows everything: pending approvals, confirmed events, and what AI will create. No hunting across tabs.
  • Progressive disclosure — Start simple (one-click), reveal complexity only when needed (inline editor).
  • No dead ends — Every screen has clear actions. "What can I do here?" is always obvious.
  • AI transparency — "Why this itinerary?" shows AI reasoning. Users understand and trust the suggestions.
  • Inline over modals — Edit in place. No jarring modal opens/closes. Keep context visible.
  • Smart defaults — AI fills everything, user just reviews. Change only what you want.
  • Clear status — Draft vs. Pending vs. Confirmed. Always know where an event stands.
  • Escape hatches — "Skip this week", "Build from scratch" — always a way out when AI doesn't fit.

Problems This Solves

  • "Too many steps" → One-click creation with smart defaults. 2-3 steps max.
  • "Hard to understand" → AI explains its reasoning. Clear labels for everything.
  • "Lacks visibility" → Timeline shows all events: past, present, future, and what AI will do.
  • "Buttons go nowhere" → Every action leads somewhere. Clear status and next steps.
  • "Not enough control" → Inline editor lets you change anything. AI suggestions, not mandates.
  • "Clunky/friction" → No tab switching, no modals, no "which path do I take?" decisions.
