# Kinmo: What It Actually Is

## The Real Problem

Planning to see friends isn't hard. Doing it over and over is exhausting.

Every group has someone who always texts "when are you free?" That person gets tired. When they stop, the group stops. Not because anyone's mad. Just inertia.

---

## Why Groups Fade

### Someone has to be the planner
Every group has 1-2 people who make things happen. They send the texts, pick the place, herd the cats. Eventually they burn out or move or get busy. Nobody picks up the slack. The group drifts.

### Fear of being annoying
You want to reach out but worry they're too busy. So you wait. They're thinking the same thing. Months go by.

### The "maybe" spiral
A few "can't make it"s and people stop asking. Nobody's upset — just nobody's inviting anymore either.

### Receivers vs. initiators
Some people reach out, some people show up when invited. Two receivers can genuinely enjoy each other and still never hang out — because neither initiates.

---

## What Kinmo Does

Kinmo is the planner who doesn't get tired.

- Finds times that work for the group
- Suggests places based on what the group actually likes
- Sends reminders so things don't fall through
- Keeps nudging even when life gets busy
- Doesn't take "maybe" personally

It's not profound. It's just useful. The annoying logistics part, automated.

---

## The Point

Groups that plan more, see each other more. Obvious, but true.

Kinmo removes the friction so the default is "yes, let's do something" instead of "we should really hang out sometime" (for the 20th time).

---

## One-Liners

**Positioning:** AI that handles the annoying parts of group planning.

**For the skeptic:** Less "we should hang out," more "see you Saturday."

**The honest pitch:** Someone has to be the planner. Now it doesn't have to be you.
